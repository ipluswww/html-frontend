    Toaster ovens come in an abundant variety of shapes, styles, and options in the marketplace. It can be difficult to select the version that will fit your family best.

    Toaster Oven Features

    Home chefs have access to plenty of heating mechanisms, including ovens, stovetops and microwaves.The toaster oven combines the best of those tools. Here are some factors you can use to evaluate the ovens on your short list.

    Temperature Range

    You need the flexibility to lightly toast a piece of bread at a low temperature or heat up mini-pizzas for your family with significantly more heat. Toaster ovens have a variety of temperature settings, some as high as 500°F.

    DID YOU KNOW?

    Toaster ovens typically range in temperature from 150F to 500F.

    Features

    Cook Settings

    Each oven has a unique combination of settings for the perfect bake. Options include broil, toast, convection bake, and warm. Some toaster ovens offer food-specific settings, such as temperatures perfectly suited to pizza, cookies, or toast.

    Features

    Capacity

    Determine how much space is on the inside of your toaster oven. If you have a small family, you might need less cubic feet. If you plan to feed a lot of people with your toaster oven, look for a model that is bigger with more shelf space.

    EXPERT TIP

    Check the capacity of your toaster oven to ensure it can comfortably house the food you enjoy. You dont want food to become misshapen because you have to force it into the interior.

    Features

    Visibility

    How easy is to it see inside the toaster oven to check on your food? Most toaster ovens have a broad glass panel that makes it simple to check in on what you’re heating. But keep in mind that with wear and tear over time, the glass could become clouded. Read product-specific reviews to determine which models are best-suited for longtime use.

    Always use oven mitts when handling foods in the toaster oven. They are just as hot as a typical oven.
    Features

    Timer Settings

    The primary function of a toaster oven timer is to ensure you don’t overcook or burn your food. Some ovens have preset functions, such as a 30-minute timer for defrost and longer timers for specific types of food. It’s nice to have several options, but at the end of the day, the main question is whether you can quickly begin the cooking process with a simple turn of a knob.

    Features

    Trays and Racks

    How easy is it to pull trays and racks out of the oven, particularly at high temperatures? If a tray snags on the edge of the oven, it could be a safety hazard to yank it out. Find a model with trays and racks that come out effortlessly.

    Racks are an important consideration, with most models ranging from one rack to three for cooking and baking flexibility.

    How Toaster Ovens Work

    Toaster ovens typically provide two primary cooking methods. The first is the toaster component located at the top of the machine. Depending on the size of your oven, you can typically toast between two and four pieces of bread. The toast is slotted vertically into the machine for rapid heating and browning.

    The second component is the oven component, which is where most of the variety, settings, and knobs can be found. The primary tools at the front of a toaster oven include a large glass door with a handle that can be pulled out and down to make space to place your food inside.

    Many ovens have a detachable tray for crumbs at the bottom of the interior. Above this tray are one to three racks for placing food horizontally for heating. You can place toast in here, as well as heftier foods like pizza.

    Knobs or buttons on the front of the machine help you regulate the type of cooking you are doing, the temperature of the cook, and how long the cook takes. Simply preheat the oven to the proper temperature, place your food in the oven, and set the timer for it to begin to cook.

    Standard or high-tech? Todays toaster ovens allow you to choose between digital display screens or more common manual knobs.

    Toaster Oven Lifespans

    A good toaster oven is built to last. These appliances can function at a high level for years, even up to a decade or more. With the possibility of keeping your toaster oven a long time, you may want to consider a few things:

    • Check reviews of the model you’re considering. Do other owners indicate this model lasts a long time?

    • What design style fits into your kitchen decor?

    • Do you prefer an LCD display, or are simple labeled knobs sufficient?

    • What color and finish are most appealing to you?

    EXPERT TIP

    Consider how your appliances complement one another. A toaster oven cant cook big pot roasts or 3-ft.-wide pizzas, but its fantastic for smaller and more manageable portion sizes.

    Getting the Most Out of a Toaster Oven

    Maintenance

    Keep your oven clean

    Most ovens have a collection tray at the bottom for dried crumbs and food specks. Once per week, remove the tray and discard the crumbs. Wash it by hand or in the top rack of your dishwasher before replacing it in the oven.

    EXPERT TIP

    Use plastic to remove crumbs and food debris from your toaster oven. Never insert metal utensils in the oven.

    Staff  | BestReviews
    Maintenance

    Carefully clear debris

    Large pieces of trapped food will burn, causing bad odors or worse – fire. If big chunks of bread become trapped inside the appliance or wedged in the toaster, unplug the appliance and gently shake out the crumbs.

    Maintenance

    Use plastic utensils, not metal

    Always use plastic utensils to extract stubborn crumbs or larger pieces of food. And always unplug your toaster oven before cleaning or performing any kind of repair. Never stick a metal utensil in your toaster oven. Metal tools can scratch the interior, and, of course, a metal instrument in an electric appliance can produce a nasty shock.

    EXPERT TIP

    Always unplug your toaster oven before extracting trapped food or giving the oven a routine cleaning.

    FAQ

    Q. Why would I choose a toaster oven over a conventional toaster?

    A. A conventional toaster only has slots for bread, whereas a toaster oven opens up new cooking possibilities for pizza and more. You can also place toast flat and shred cheese or other complementary ingredients on top of it for melting.

    Q. When should I use a toaster oven instead of a regular oven?

    A. A regular oven can take a long time to heat and is best-suited for big multi-course meals or occasions when there is more food than space in a toaster oven. A toaster oven, by contrast, is ideally suited for quick meals, rapid heating, and smaller portions. The beauty of a toaster oven is the ability to select from an array of times and temperature settings, and to rapidly move baked or cooked food in or out to feed family members gathered around the dinner table.
